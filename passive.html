Passive voice occurs when the subject of a sentence is acted upon by the verb. It's often used when the focus is on the action rather than the doer.

Subject + Form of "to be" + Past Participle + (by + Agent)

  • Active: The chef cooked the meal.
  • Passive: The meal was cooked by the chef.
  • Active: Scientists discovered the vaccine.
  • Passive: The vaccine was discovered by scientists.
